01 The Problem

Not a visibility problem. A relevance problem.

The F&B market in Tier-3 Indian cities is simultaneously oversaturated and underleveraged. Chai shops exist on every corner — but almost none of them have a brand. The real problem ran deeper than being unknown.

The Diagnostic Insight
"The problem was not that nobody knew about ChaiVours. The problem was that even if they did know, they had no reason to feel anything about it. Brand strategy had to solve for identity before it solved for awareness."
❌
No Differentiation

Every chai shop in a Tier-3 city claims to be good. Product quality alone gives no reason to choose one brand over another. ChaiVours needed a reason beyond the chai.

❌
No Cultural Fit

Most F&B brands in smaller cities either look like corporate chains or feel completely generic. The Gen-Z and college audience had no brand in this category that spoke their language or reflected their identity.

❌
No Community

Without a loyal group of customers who feel ownership over the brand, a chai shop is just a transactional space. The goal had to be making ChaiVours feel like a place — an identity — not just a shop.

02 Strategic Insight

Build the tribe first. Sell second.

One insight shaped every decision that followed — and it came directly from the audience research.

The Core Insight
The audience does not return to a chai shop because the chai is better. They return because the place feels like them. Brand loyalty in this category is driven by identity, not product.
💡
What this meant for content

Do not lead with product. Lead with identity. Cultural content had to come first — building a brand personality the audience felt was theirs — before a single product post was published.

📱
What this meant for the platform

Instagram was the only platform that mattered. This is where the 17–25 audience discovers, expresses identity, and shares experiences. Every rupee of creative effort went here.

04 Brand Positioning

ChaiVours is not just a chai shop.

It is the hangout brand for young people who want a space that feels like them — warm, local, and real. Every content decision was made through this lens.

Tone of Voice

Gen-Z, regional humour, casual and warm. Every caption sounds like a friend texting — not a brand posting. Never corporate. Never generic.

Visual Identity

Bold colours, food-first imagery, meme-friendly formats. Every post looks native to Instagram — designed to feel like content, not an ad.

Content Feel

Like a friend's Instagram account, not a business running ads. Never salesy. Always relatable. The moment it feels like a brand pushing product, it loses.

Core Emotion

Belonging. This place is yours. The content reinforces that identity at every touchpoint — so the audience feels ownership before they even visit.

Content Pillars

Six pillars. Every post had a job.

Every piece of content published for ChaiVours was mapped to one of six defined pillars. This ensured the brand never drifted into random posting.

Pillar 01
Culture & Identity

Gen-Z tone, regional humour, meme formats. Makes the audience feel the brand is one of them. The highest-reach pillar.

Pillar 02
Product Spotlight

Hero SKU content — Elaichi Chai, Zinger Burger, seasonal specials. Always shown in context of a hangout, never as a plain product shot.

Pillar 03
Behind the Scenes

The team, the kitchen, the prep. Makes the audience feel like insiders. Builds warmth and trust at the same time.

Pillar 04
User Generated Content

Reposting customer tags and visits. The most powerful trust signal. Shows real people choosing the brand without being asked.

Pillar 05
Local & Seasonal

City-specific content, local events, festival tie-ins. Reinforces that this brand belongs to this specific community.

Pillar 06
Engagement

Questions, polls, this-or-that formats. Keeps the audience active and builds a sense of conversation with the brand.

05 Content Funnel

From stranger to brand ambassador.

This was not random posting. Every content type served a specific stage designed to move the audience from first discovery all the way to becoming a loyal advocate who brings others in.

Top Funnel — Awareness
Trend-aligned Reels · Meme formats · Regional humour · City-specific content · Relatable Gen-Z situations
Objective: Get discovered. Make the brand visible to people who have never heard of ChaiVours.
Cultural content travels. Funny, relatable, or locally relevant content gets shared by people who have not visited yet. This is how strangers find the brand.
Mid Funnel — Consideration
Product Spotlight posts · Behind the Scenes content · Staff and ambience videos · Customer visit content
Objective: Convert curious followers into people who actively want to visit.
Once someone follows, they need reasons to want to come in. Showing the product, the people, and the vibe builds the desire to experience it in person.
Conversion — Footfall
Location tags · Limited-time menu items · Special combos · Event-based content (chai with friends, study sessions)
Objective: Turn digital engagement into a physical visit to the store.
Content that creates urgency or FOMO drives the final step from follower to customer. A new flavour or a limited item gives people a reason to come in now.
Retention & Advocacy — UGC Engine
Resharing UGC · Celebrating regulars · Making customers feel seen and special
Objective: Turn customers into brand advocates who bring others in for free.
When customers are reshared or celebrated, they feel ownership of the brand. They tag friends, bring groups, and become the brand's most powerful marketing channel — at zero cost.
06 Execution

Built everything from scratch.

Strategy without execution is just a document. Seven things were built, created, and published to bring the ChaiVours brand strategy to life — every element executed alone.

01Audit
Brand Audit & Diagnosis

Before creating anything, I audited what existed — the physical brand, visual style, existing customer base, and competitive landscape. I interviewed the client about who their regulars were, what SKUs sold best offline, and what they wanted the brand to feel like. This became the foundation for everything.

02Research
Audience Research

Mapped the target audience across Instagram by studying competitor accounts in similar Tier-3 markets, analysing comment sections, and observing what content local youth engaged with. Built a detailed ICP covering demographics, psychographics, online behaviour, trust signals, and purchase triggers.

03Positioning
Brand Positioning

Defined the brand positioning — ChaiVours as a cultural hangout identity, not just a chai shop. Wrote the brand voice guidelines (Gen-Z tone, regional humour, warm and casual), defined the 6 content pillars, and created the content framework that all future posts would follow.

04Mapping
SKU Identification & Content Mapping

Analysed offline sales data with the client to identify the top-selling products. Mapped each hero SKU to a content format — product spotlight videos, before-and-after prep content, and aesthetic flat-lay posts. Critically, product content was held back until cultural positioning content had built enough brand equity first.

05Publishing
Content Production & Publishing

Produced and published 170+ posts across the ChaiVours Instagram account. This included trend-aligned Reels, product spotlight posts, behind-the-scenes content, seasonal content, and engagement formats. Each post was written in the defined brand voice and designed to serve a specific funnel stage.

06UGC
UGC Activation

As the brand grew, customers began organically tagging ChaiVours in their posts. Created a system to identify, curate, and reshare the best UGC — making customers feel celebrated and seen. The more we reshared, the more others tagged, creating a self-sustaining content engine at zero cost.

07Optimise
Performance Review & Optimisation

Reviewed content performance weekly — analysing which formats drove the most reach, saves, and profile visits. Underperforming content types were deprioritised and replaced with formats that resonated. This iterative approach ensured the content system improved over time rather than staying static.

08 Key Learnings

What this actually taught me.

Four learnings from this project that apply to any brand in any category.

Cultural positioning before product push

The most important decision was to build brand identity before showing products. Brands that lead with product in crowded categories get ignored. Brands that lead with identity get followed.

UGC is the most powerful content type in F&B

When real customers tag a brand, it communicates something no paid ad can — that real people genuinely chose this place. Building a system to activate and celebrate UGC was the highest-ROI action in the entire strategy.

Consistency compounds

170+ posts published with a consistent voice and identity creates a cumulative brand impression that no single viral post can replicate. The audience began to recognise ChaiVours' tone before they even read the caption.

Audience research before content creation — always

The entire strategy was built on the insight that the problem was cultural relevance, not visibility. Without the research, we would have defaulted to generic product posts and seen minimal results.

What I Would Do Next
  • Introduce a WhatsApp community for regulars — highest retention channel at zero cost.
  • Run a referral content campaign: tag a friend who needs chai = one free cup. Activates word-of-mouth at scale.
  • Test YouTube Shorts with behind-the-scenes content — cross-platform reach with same production effort.
