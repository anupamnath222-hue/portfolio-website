01 The Problem

A crowded market. No room for the small guy. Or so they thought.

Three real problems stopped the brand before it could start. Understanding them precisely is what made every strategic decision after this one possible.

❌
Scale was impossible

National giants like Unacademy and Byju's already owned attention. Competing on budget or reach was not an option.

❌
Trust was broken

Tier-3 students don't buy from brands they don't know. They buy from faces they trust. There was no face yet.

❌
No clear identity

The brand "Learning Point" was uninspired, generic, and forgettable. It looked like every other local education brand in every Tier-3 city.

02 Strategic Insight

Competing on scale was unrealistic. Competing on trust was the only viable path.

One insight shaped every decision that followed.

The Core Insight
In a Tier-3 education market, the audience does not trust big brands. They trust local faces, real results, and consistent proof. Authority first. Conversion second.
💡
What this meant for the brand

Stop trying to look like a national brand. Look like a person. Build authority through daily value before asking for anything. The content had to earn the sale before the course page existed.

🎯
What this meant for the name

Learning Point was generic. It told people nothing about the promise. PrepIQ positioned the brand as the smart prep choice — signalled intelligence, specificity, and a clear category. A name is positioning.

03 Target Audience

Who we were really talking to.

Not a demographic. A specific person with a specific fear at a specific moment in their life.

👤
The Profile

Age 18–26. Tier-3 cities, North and Northeast India. Preparing for government exams. Goal: a government job for family financial security.

😱
The Fear

Wasting years and still failing. Every rupee spent is a family decision. Price-sensitive. Every purchase is a bet on their future.

💎
Trust Signal

Real results. Local faces. Peer proof. Not a celebrity endorsement or a polished ad — a neighbour who actually cleared the exam.

📱
Where They Live Online

Facebook Groups (local exam prep communities), YouTube (free Hindi GK content), Instagram (motivation reels), WhatsApp (exam notifications, peer forwarding).

04 Content Funnel

Every piece of content had a job.

Content was not created to fill a calendar. Every post mapped to a specific funnel stage and served a specific conversion goal.

Top Funnel — Awareness
GK questions · Current affairs · Govt job notifications → Posted DAILY on Facebook and Instagram
Goal: Build authority and habit. Become the daily source they trust. Own a slot in their routine before they know who you are.
Mid Funnel — Consideration
Video-based organic lead magnets → Free value, deep preparation content
Goal: Convert casual followers into serious prospects. Give enough that they believe you have more to give.
Conversion — Revenue
Paid course launch timed to exam window → ₹1,500–₹2,000 per enrollment
Goal: Convert trust into revenue. No discounts needed. The audience already knew, liked, and trusted the brand before the offer landed.
05 What I Actually Did

Built everything from scratch.

Six steps. One person. No agency. No budget.

01 Research
Audience Research

Mapped the ICP across Facebook groups, YouTube comments, and WhatsApp communities to understand language, fears, and motivators. The vocabulary the audience used became the vocabulary the brand used.

02 Positioning
Brand Positioning & Rebrand

Identified trust as the only real competitive advantage. Rebranded from Learning Point to PrepIQ. Built an authority-first content system around daily GK, exam updates, and study tips that made the brand feel local, credible, and consistent.

03 Funnel
Funnel Design

Mapped every content piece to a funnel stage. Top (awareness and habit building) → Mid (proof and consideration) → Conversion (paid course launch). Nothing was posted without a clear answer to: what job does this do?

04 Execution
Content Execution

Created and published daily content on Facebook and Instagram. Lead magnet videos produced for mid-funnel. Zero paid promotion throughout. Every view and follower was earned.

05 Launch
Course Launch

Timed the paid course launch to the exam window. Priced at ₹1,500–₹2,000. 25 students enrolled organically in the first launch. Not a single rupee spent on ads. The trust that was built over months did the selling.

06 Proof
Post-Launch Proof

Five students cleared the government exam. Used their results as social proof to strengthen brand credibility for future launches. Real outcomes became the next campaign. The content strategy fed itself.
